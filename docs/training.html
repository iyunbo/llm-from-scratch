Pretraining

Chapter 5 — Training the GPT model from scratch with next-token prediction, learning rate scheduling, and checkpointing.

Training Pipeline

Learning Rate Schedule

Training uses a two-phase schedule: linear warmup for the first 10% of steps (avoids instability from random initial weights), then cosine decay that smoothly reduces the learning rate to a minimum value.

ParameterValueWhy
Peak LR5e-4Standard for small-scale GPT training
Min LR1e-5Prevents learning from stopping completely
Warmup10% of stepsStabilizes early training with random weights
Grad clipmax_norm=1.0Prevents exploding gradients
Weight decay0.1Regularization (skipped for bias and LayerNorm)

Optimizer: AdamW

AdamW applies weight decay directly to weights rather than through the gradient (unlike L2 regularization in vanilla Adam). The implementation selectively excludes bias and LayerNorm parameters from weight decay, since regularizing them hurts performance.

Evaluation Metrics

Cross-Entropy Loss Measures how well the model predicts the next token
Perplexity exp(loss) — roughly "how many tokens is the model choosing between"

A random baseline has perplexity ≈ vocab_size (50,257). Good LLMs achieve 10–30 on standard benchmarks. Lower is better.

Checkpointing

The training loop periodically saves model weights, optimizer state, and metadata (epoch, step, loss) to .pt files. This allows resuming interrupted training and selecting the best checkpoint.
